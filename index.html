SEMINAR'17

January 2017
3D PASSWORD

3D Password is a multifactor authentication scheme. It combines recognition, recall, tokens, biometrics in one authentication system. The authentication can be improved with 3D password, because the unauthorised person may not interact with same object at a particular location as the legitimate user. It is difficult to crack, because it has fixed number of steps and procedure. Added with biometrics and token verification this schema becomes almost unbreakable.

MINOR PROJECT'16

September 2016 - December 2016
ANTITHEFT SYSTEMS

An anti-theft system is any device or method used to prevent or deter the unauthorized appropriation of items considered valuable. Theft is one of the most common and oldest criminal behavior, from the invention of the first lock and key to the introduction of NFC tags and biometric identification, anti-theft systems have evolved to match the introduction of new inventions to society and the resulting theft of them by others.

Ownership is often indicated by means of visual marking (license plates, name tags). When clear owner identification is not possible and when there is a lack of social observance, people may be inclined to take possession of items to their own benefit at the expense of the original owner. Motive and opportunity are two enabling factors for theft. Given that motives for theft are varied and complex and are generally speaking not within the control of the victim, most methods of theft prevention rely on reducing opportunities for theft.
